Dethroning King Dollar: How BRICS+ Plans to Rewire Global Trade with "The Unit"

For nearly eight decades, the US dollar has been the undisputed heavyweight champion of global finance. But a new digital challenger is stepping into the ring.

The Dollar Toll Booth vs. The BRICS Expressway: Visualizing the shift from high-friction dollar settlements to streamlined digital corridors.

Since the end of World War II, the US dollar has been the dominant currency in global trade. Whether it's Brazil buying German machinery or India purchasing Saudi oil, the transactions are almost always priced and paid for in greenbacks.

This dominance grants the US significant geopolitical leverage. Because these dollars eventually pass through the US banking system, Washington has the power to freeze a country's access to its own funds—effectively "muting" an entire economy if its actions fall out of favor.

Key Takeaway

The dollar isn't just a currency; it's a global operating system that the US currently controls the 'admin' rights to.

However, the status quo is facing its most credible challenge yet. The BRICS+ nations (Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa, and their new allies) have launched "The Unit," a digital settlement currency pilot phase initiated in March 2026. Its primary objective? To bypass the US dollar entirely.

What is "The Unit"?

"The Unit" isn't something you'll use to buy a cup of coffee. It is a wholesale cross-border mechanism designed exclusively for central banks and large financial institutions to settle multi-billion-dollar trade deals.

To understand its significance, we need to distinguish between two systems: SWIFT (the secure messaging system global banks use to talk to each other) and the Settlement System (the actual plumbing that moves the money). "The Unit" aims to provide an alternative to both.

The Global Context: De-dollarization

The motivation is simple: Survival. The 2022 sanctions against Russia—where Western nations removed Russian banks from SWIFT and froze billions in foreign reserves—served as a clarion call. For many emerging economies, the dollar shifted from being a "convenient tool" to a "potential weapon."

"The world is fracturing. Global liquidity is expected to split between the existing Western system and the new BRICS+ ledger."

The Indian Context: Oil vs. The Dragon

For India, "The Unit" is a double-edged sword. On one hand, the Opportunities are massive. As one of the world's largest oil importers, India pays hefty conversion fees and faces exchange rate risks when buying in dollars. Moving to "The Unit" could save billions in foreign exchange reserves.

On the other hand, there's The Dragon in the Room. Given China’s economic weight, New Delhi fears "The Unit" could simply replace dollar dominance with Yuan dominance.

Strategic Balance: India must navigate the delicate task of lowering trade costs while ensuring that this new digital currency doesn't become a vehicle for regional hegemony. Integrating the "India Stack" (UPI) with "The Unit" remains a top priority for policymakers.

The Verdict

The US dollar isn't going to vanish overnight. Habits are hard to break, and the liquidity of the US Treasury market is unparalleled. However, the launch of "The Unit" signals the definitive end of the dollar's absolute monopoly.

Welcome to the era of financial multipolarity.
